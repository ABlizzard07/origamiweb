Interesting Facts About Camels

  • There are two types of camels: one humped dromedary camels, and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels sleep standing up, for about six hours a night.
  • A camel's foot has two toes that spread to stop them from sinking in the sand.

Interesting Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons can quickly change color based on the temperature, environment, or their mood.
  • The chameleon's tongue can accelerate very quickly in order to catch prey.
  • Most chameleons live in Madagascar.

Interesting Facts About Cicadas

  • The cicada is the loudest bug in the world.
  • Most cicadas have red-orange eyes.
  • There are two groups, dog-day cicadas and periodical cicadas.

Interesting Facts About Pandas

  • Their fur is waterproof.
  • Panda cubs are born blind.
  • They have an extra digit, which is like a thumb, to grasp food.

Interesting Facts About Pigeons

  • They were the first birds that humans domesticated.
  • They see the world like a kaleidoscope.
  • They do backflips in midair.

Interesting Facts About Bears

  • The smallest bear in the world is the sun bear.
  • Bears have never lived in Australia or Antarctica.
  • Canada has 60% of the world's polar bears.
